Course Policies

Attendance

Because of the fast-paced nature of this course, attendance and active participation are vital to your learning.

Deadlines

Projects are due a week after they are assigned, except when otherwise noted on the project page. Projects are due at 11:59 PM on their due date, with a ten minute grace period due to technical issues with submission.

Collaboration

I expect students to work together on the programming assignments in this course. However, there are some restrictions that I feel are reasonable on inter-student collaboration. For any programming assignment, students may opt to use pair-programming, as described below. Your programming partner is the only person with whom you are permitted to share code. If you choose not to pair-program, you may neither share your code nor look at the code of others in the course.

Pair Programming

Pair Programming is a technique for programming in which two programmers collaborate on a program together on one machine. That is, the two programmers share a screen and a keyboard, switching control at their discretion. There are many ways to divide labor in pair programming, but the way I recommend is the Driver-Navigator paradigm. In this paradigm, one programmer uses the keyboard and writes code, while the other observes each line as it is written. The two should maintain a constant dialogue, with the driver explaining what he or she is doing, while the navigator comments on the overall structure and brings up mistakes and errors. If you choose to pair-program, you must not ever work on the code without your partner. Further, pair-programming does not mean dividing work. No code should ever be written without your partner directly observing.

When it comes time to submit your work, you and your partner will submit one copy of the assignment together, noting who makes up your pair.

Grading

While this course is Pass-Fail, performance on assignments will be assessed using a range-oriented grading scheme. The five possible grades are, in order from most desirable to least desirable, Excellent, Very Good, Good, Fair, and Poor,

An additional grade, No Credit, indicates that the assignment was not completed or was ungradeable.

In general, assignments will be assessed on several factors. A write-up, for instance, will receive a separate grade from a coding question even if both appear in the same assignment. Regarding coding questions specifically, work will be assessed both for functional correctness and readability.

Grade Breakdown

  • Nine Projects: 60%
  • Two Quizzes: 10% (5% each)
  • One Group Presentation: 5%
  • Class Participation: 25%
